Mitochondria are organelles that are common to all eukaryotes. They are the "powerhouses" of the cell. They preform cellular respiration and produce ATP for the cell to use.

[INSERT MITOCHONDRIA DIAGRAM HERE]

Mitochondria are made up of two phospholipid bilayers, the same kind of membrane as the one around the whole cell. There is one outer membrane, seperating the organelle from the rest of the cell, and one inner membrane, used for cellular respiration. The inner membrane has folds, as can be seen above, to maximize urface area.

The space inside of the inner membrane is called the matrix. The matrix is full of ribosomes, matrix granules, and proteins used for cellular respiration.

Mitochondria have their own DNA
